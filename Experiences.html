Welcome to CBEA Experiences 🌎

Welcome to Corozal Bay EcoAdventures (CBEA) — Belize's premier community-based ecotourism operator, based in the beautiful coastal village of Sarteneja, Corozal District. Every experience we offer is designed to connect you with Belize's extraordinary natural and cultural heritage, while directly supporting local families and the conservation of the Corozal Bay Wildlife Sanctuary and Bacalar Chico National Park. From mangrove restoration to Maya ruins, snorkelling UNESCO reefs to crafting lionfish jewellery — every adventure here is authentic, meaningful, and unforgettable. 🍃

🌿 Mangrove Restoration & New River Expedition ★ Most Popular

Mangrove Restoration is CBEA's flagship experience — a full-day journey along the ancient Maya trade route of the New River, one of northern Belize's most ecologically rich waterways. SACD conservation experts guide you through a deep educational session on the vital role mangroves play as natural storm barriers, carbon stores, and marine nurseries. Each participant then prepares and plants their own seedlings in the Warrie Bight coastal restoration zone — making a direct, lasting contribution to Belize's shoreline.

From BZ$210 / person ⏱ Full Day — 6 to 8 hours

🌿 What's Included

  • ✔ Boat journey through the New River's ecological corridor
  • ✔ Educational session with SACD conservation experts
  • ✔ Hands-on mangrove seedling preparation & coastal planting
  • ✔ Species identification — migratory birds and crocodiles
  • ✔ Learn how mangroves protect coastlines, store carbon, and nursery marine life
  • ✔ All equipment and safety gear provided

🦋 Snorkelling — Bacalar Chico Marine Reserve

Bacalar Chico Marine Reserve is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most biodiverse reef ecosystems in the entire Caribbean. CBEA guides take you through crystal-clear waters teeming with coral formations and colourful marine species. A highlight of this tour is crossing the historic artificial channel that marks the territorial boundary between Belize and Mexico — a fascinating intersection of nature and history.

From BZ$558 / person ⏱ Full Day — 7 to 9 hours

🦋 What's Included

  • ✔ Guided snorkel over coral formations & diverse marine life
  • ✔ UNESCO World Heritage Site — Bacalar Chico Marine Reserve
  • ✔ Cross the historic Belize–Mexico territorial boundary channel
  • ✔ On-site museum visit for historical & cultural context
  • ✔ Responsible no-fins reef protocol to protect coral
  • ✔ Life jackets and all safety equipment provided

🎣 Traditional PescaTours Fishing

PescaTours invites you to join Sarteneja's fishing families at sunrise for an authentic dawn-to-afternoon voyage on the bay. You will learn ancestral fishing techniques — stingray-trap use, net casting, and line fishing — methods passed carefully down through generations. The morning ends at a local family home where the fresh catch is prepared into a traditional Belizean lunch right before your eyes.

From BZ$258 / person ⏱ Half to Full Day — 5 to 7 hours

🎣 What's Included

  • ✔ Early morning departure from Sarteneja's main dock
  • ✔ Ancestral stingray-trap, net casting & line fishing techniques
  • ✔ Sustainable catch management hands-on demonstration
  • ✔ Traditional Belizean lunch cooked with the morning's fresh catch
  • ✔ Local storytelling and cultural exchange with fishing families
  • ✔ Directly supports 7 local PescaTours fishing families

🏭 Maya Archaeological Zone Tour

Belize's ancient Maya civilisation shaped every corner of this coastline. CBEA takes you to two complementary sites — the coastal trade hub of Cerros, once a key maritime centre where goods like cacao, jade, and obsidian were traded between Belize and Mexico, and the magnificent Altun Ha, one of Belize's most celebrated archaeological treasures. Climb majestic pyramids, explore ceremonial plazas, and hear the stories of a civilisation that flourished here for millennia.

From BZ$170 / person ⏱ Full Day — 7 to 8 hours

🏭 What's Included

  • ✔ Guided visit to Cerros — ancient coastal Maya maritime trade centre
  • ✔ Exploration of Altun Ha's temples, plazas & carved monuments
  • ✔ Expert local guide with deep cultural and historical knowledge
  • ✔ Learn how the New River served as an ancient Maya trade artery
  • ✔ Photography opportunities at both UNESCO-recognised sites
  • ✔ Transport between both sites included

🦇 Wildtracks Manatee Rescue Centre

Wildtracks is Belize's leading rehabilitation facility for endangered manatees and primates — and one of CBEA's most treasured partner organisations. During this behind-the-scenes visit, the Wildtracks scientific team shares their specialised conservation and rehabilitation methodologies. Participants get hands-on involvement, assisting in seagrass collection — the primary food source for manatees — and observing feeding sessions with rehabilitating animals up close.

Included in group packages ⏱ Half Day — 3 to 4 hours

🦇 What's Included

  • ✔ Guided tour of manatee & primate rehabilitation facility
  • ✔ Learn specialist conservation and rehabilitation methodologies
  • ✔ Hands-on seagrass collection for manatee feeding
  • ✔ Observe feeding sessions with rehabilitating manatees
  • ✔ Q&A with Wildtracks' internationally recognised science team
  • ✔ Understand the link between seagrass health and manatee survival

✨ Lionfish Jewellery Workshop

In the waters of Belize, the lionfish poses a serious ecological threat — preying on native species and damaging vital coral reefs. In response, a collective of Sarteneja's women artisans developed an extraordinary solution: they transform lionfish fins, sourced directly from local fishers, into stunning handcrafted jewellery — earrings, necklaces, and bracelets known for their beauty and uniqueness.

From BZ$90 / person ⏱ 2 to 3 hours

✨ What's Included

  • ✔ Learn about lionfish's ecological threat to Caribbean reefs
  • ✔ Hands-on jewellery crafting with local women artisans
  • ✔ Design and take home your own unique handcrafted piece
  • ✔ Fins sourced directly from Sarteneja's fishing community
  • ✔ Directly supports 3 women-led artisan families
  • ✔ Contributes to ongoing invasive species reef conservation efforts
